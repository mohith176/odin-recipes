Biryani Recipe

Description

Biryani is a flavorful and aromatic rice dish that originates from the Indian subcontinent. To prepare it, start by marinating chicken, mutton, or vegetables in a mixture of yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt for at least 30 minutes. Meanwhile, parboil basmati rice with whole spices like bay leaves, cardamom, cinnamon, and cloves until it's 70% cooked. In a large pot, layer the marinated meat or vegetables with the partially cooked rice. Add fried onions, fresh mint, cilantro, and a drizzle of saffron-infused milk for richness. Seal the pot with a tight lid or dough to trap steam and cook on low heat (dum cooking) for 20-30 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld. Serve hot with raita or salad for a complete meal.

Ingredients

  • 4 tablespoons vegetable oil, divided - Used for frying and cooking the ingredients.
  • 4 small potatoes, peeled and halved - Adds a hearty texture to the biryani.
  • 2 large onions, finely chopped - Provides a sweet and caramelized flavor base.
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced - Adds a pungent, aromatic flavor.
  • 1 tablespoon minced fresh ginger root - Enhances the dish with a warm, spicy note.
  • 2 medium tomatoes, peeled and chopped - Adds tanginess and moisture to the dish.
  • 1 teaspoon salt - For seasoning.
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin - Adds an earthy, nutty flavor.
  • ½ teaspoon chili powder - Provides heat and color.
  • ½ teaspoon ground black pepper - Adds a mild spiciness.
  • ½ teaspoon ground turmeric - Gives a vibrant yellow color and earthy flavor.
  • 2 tablespoons plain yogurt - Tenderizes the chicken and adds creaminess.
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh mint leaves - Adds a refreshing, aromatic touch.
  • ½ teaspoon ground cardamom - Provides a sweet, floral flavor.
  • 1 (2 inch) piece cinnamon stick - Adds warmth and depth to the dish.
  • 3 pounds boneless, skinless chicken pieces, cut into chunks - The main protein for the biryani.

Rice

  • 1 pound basmati rice - The long-grain rice that gives biryani its signature fragrance and texture.
  • 2 ½ tablespoons vegetable oil - Used for sautéing and cooking the rice and spices.
  • 1 large onion, diced - Adds sweetness and depth to the dish when caramelized.
  • 5 pods cardamom - Provides a sweet, floral aroma to the rice.
  • 3 whole cloves - Adds a warm, slightly bitter flavor.
  • 1 (1 inch) piece cinnamon stick - Infuses the rice with a warm, woody flavor.
  • ½ teaspoon ground ginger - Adds a spicy, earthy note to the rice.
  • 1 pinch powdered saffron - Adds a luxurious aroma and vibrant color to the rice.
  • 4 cups chicken stock - Cooks the rice and adds rich flavor.
  • 1 ½ teaspoons salt - Seasons the rice to perfection.

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a large skillet. Fry potatoes in hot oil until lightly browned, about 3 to 5 minutes. Remove to a paper towel-lined plate to drain; set aside.
  3. Add remaining 2 tablespoons of oil to the skillet. Add onions, garlic, and fresh ginger; cook and stir until onion is soft and golden. Add tomatoes, salt, cumin, chili powder, pepper, and turmeric; cook, stirring constantly, for 5 minutes.
  4. Stir in yogurt, mint, ground cardamom, and cinnamon stick. Cover and cook over low heat, stirring occasionally, until tomatoes are cooked to a pulp. It may be necessary to add a little hot water if mixture becomes too dry and starts to stick to the pan.
  5. Add chicken and stir well to coat. Cover and cook over very low heat until chicken is tender, 35 to 45 minutes. There should only be a little very thick gravy left when chicken is finished cooking. If necessary cook uncovered for a few minutes to reduce the gravy.
  6. Meanwhile, make the rice: Wash rice well and drain in a colander for at least 30 minutes.
  7. Heat oil in a large skillet. Add onion; cook and stir until golden. Add cardamom pods, cloves, cinnamon stick, ground ginger, and saffron; stir in rice until coated with spices.
  8. Heat stock and salt in a medium pot until hot; pour over rice and stir well.
  9. Add chicken mixture and potatoes; stir gently to combine. Bring to a boil.
  10. Reduce heat to very low, cover with a tight-fitting lid, and steam for 20 minutes without lifting the lid or stirring.
  11. Spoon biryani onto a warm serving dish.

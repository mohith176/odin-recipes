Dosa Recipe

Description

Dosa is a classic South Indian dish made from a fermented batter of rice and urad dal (black gram). To prepare the batter, soak **1 cup of urad dal** and **2 cups of rice** (preferably parboiled or idli rice) separately for 4-6 hours. Grind the urad dal into a smooth, fluffy paste and the rice into a slightly coarse paste. Mix them together, add **1 teaspoon of salt**, and let the batter ferment overnight or for 8-10 hours in a warm place. Once fermented, the batter will rise and become slightly airy. To make dosas, heat a non-stick or cast-iron skillet, pour a ladleful of batter, and spread it in a circular motion to form a thin, even crepe. Drizzle oil or ghee around the edges and cook until the dosa turns golden and crispy. Serve hot with **coconut chutney**, **sambar**, or **potato masala** for a delicious meal! For variation, you can add spices, onions, or herbs to the batter.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup urad dal (black gram) - Provides the batter with a soft, fluffy texture.
  • 2 cups rice (preferably parboiled or idli rice) - Gives the dosa its structure and crispiness.
  • 1 teaspoon salt - Enhances the flavor of the batter.
  • Water (as needed) - For soaking and grinding the ingredients.
  • Oil or ghee - For cooking the dosa and making it crispy.

Steps

  1. Soak the ingredients: Wash and soak 1 cup urad dal and 2 cups rice (and 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds, if using) separately in water for 4-6 hours.
  2. Grind the batter: Drain the water and grind the urad dal into a smooth, fluffy paste. Grind the rice into a slightly coarse paste. Mix both pastes together in a large bowl.
  3. Add salt: Add 1 teaspoon salt to the batter and mix well. The consistency should be thick but pourable (like pancake batter).
  4. Ferment the batter: Cover the bowl and let the batter ferment in a warm place for 8-10 hours or overnight. The batter will rise and become slightly airy.
  5. Prepare the skillet: Heat a non-stick or cast-iron skillet on medium heat. Lightly grease it with oil or ghee.
  6. Spread the batter: Pour a ladleful of batter onto the skillet and spread it in a circular motion to form a thin, even crepe.
  7. Cook the dosa: Drizzle a little oil or ghee around the edges. Cook until the bottom turns golden and crispy.
  8. Flip (optional): If desired, flip the dosa and cook the other side briefly. For a classic dosa, flipping is not necessary.
  9. Serve hot: Fold the dosa and serve immediately with coconut chutney, sambar, or potato masala.
